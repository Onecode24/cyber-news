The MGM Resorts hack (2023): why a phone call beat every firewall

In September 2023, the ransomware-affiliated group Scattered Spider took down MGM Resorts' casino floors, hotel key cards, and websites for over a week — not by breaking any code, but by calling the company's IT help desk. Posing as an employee, the attacker convinced a support agent to reset a login's multi-factor authentication, handing over the keys to the network. From there, they escalated privileges and eventually deployed ransomware, costing MGM an estimated $100 million.

It's a foundational story because it shows that the weakest link in security is often a trusting human process, not a technical flaw — a lesson that keeps repeating (notice the Odido breach above used the same trick). For newcomers, the practical takeaway is that "social engineering" and "vishing" (voice phishing) are just as central to real-world attacks as malware, and that verifying identity before resetting credentials is one of the cheapest, highest-value defenses an organization can put in place.
