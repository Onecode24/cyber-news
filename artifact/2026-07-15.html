The Colonial Pipeline attack (2021): one leaked password, a shut-down pipeline, and gas lines up and down the East Coast

In May 2021, the ransomware gang DarkSide broke into Colonial Pipeline — the company that carries nearly half the fuel supply for the U.S. East Coast — using a single compromised VPN password for an account that had no multi-factor authentication. In about two hours they stole 100GB of data, then deployed ransomware onto the company's business network. Colonial proactively shut down the pipeline itself to contain the damage, even though the ransomware never touched the operational control systems directly.

The six-day shutdown triggered fuel shortages and panic buying across several states, pushed gas prices to their highest level since 2014, and forced a federal emergency declaration. Colonial paid the attackers 75 bitcoin (about $4.4 million); the FBI later clawed back roughly $2.4 million of it by tracking the ransom wallet. It's a foundational story for newcomers because it shows how a single weak credential and a missing MFA setting can cascade into a national-scale crisis — and why "critical infrastructure" isn't an abstract policy term, it's gas stations running dry.
