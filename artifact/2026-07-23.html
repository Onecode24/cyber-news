🎓 Beginner Pick — Learn the Backstory

The Colonial Pipeline Attack (2021)

Not breaking news — a classic case every newcomer should know

In May 2021, a ransomware gang called DarkSide broke into Colonial Pipeline, the company that carries nearly half the fuel supply for the U.S. East Coast, using nothing more exotic than one leaked employee password on an old VPN account that didn't have multi-factor authentication. To contain the damage, Colonial shut the pipeline down itself — and that shutdown, not the hack, is what caused the gas shortages and panic-buying seen on the news.

Colonial paid roughly $4.4 million in Bitcoin to get its systems back, but the story didn't end there: the FBI managed to trace and claw back most of the ransom by following the cryptocurrency wallet the gang used, one of the first big public wins of its kind.

It's a favorite teaching example because it shows how a single weak password can cascade into national headlines, why "ransomware" is really an extortion business model rather than one virus, and why basic hygiene — MFA, and retiring unused accounts — remains the cheapest defense against attacks that make it all the way to critical infrastructure.
